# Product

## Register

product

## Users

18–32 year olds who have tried and failed with polite reminder apps. People who respond better to being called out than coddled — gym culture, study grind, productivity communities. They share memes about their own procrastination and use apps socially. Secondary: people buying it as a gift for a lazy friend. Their context when opening the app: a quick check-in (did I do the thing?), or arriving from a notification that just roasted them.

## Product Purpose

brute is a habit tracker that ROASTS you when you flake — funny, harsh, relentless AI notifications that escalate in tactic, not just volume. Underneath the comedy it is a competent habit tracker (schedules, streaks, completion logging, day/week views); the roast is the delivery layer that gets that engine in the door. Comedy sells the install (screenshottable, free marketing); behavior change and a real social witness are what retain. Success = users who keep showing up because it actually works, not just because it's funny once.

## Brand Personality

Blunt, deadpan, screenshottable. The voice is a sharp friend calling you out in a plain-spoken message — matter-of-fact call-outs of the obvious ("The gym was open all day. You knew that. You didn't go anyway."), never slang-cosplay, never cruelty. Three words: **sharp, dry, relentless**. It roasts the behavior and the excuse, never the person's worth (the Golden Rule). Emotional goal: the user feels *seen* and a little called-out, then laughs and does the thing.

## Anti-references

- **Cutesy wellness apps** (Calm, Headspace, Finch): soft pastels, rounded blobs, mascots, calming gradients. The opposite of brute's edge.
- **Corporate SaaS / productivity dashboards**: safe navy/indigo, hero-metric templates, identical icon-heading-text card grids, stock friendliness.
- **Gen-Z loud/chaotic**: neon-on-black, sticker spam, meme fonts, maximalist gradients — loud for loud's sake.
- **The default AI-generated look**: cream/beige backgrounds, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, 01/02/03 numbered section markers, gradient text, endlessly repeated identical cards.

## Design Principles

1. **The copy carries the edge; the UI stays sharp.** Restraint is the flex — a premium, controlled interface makes the mean copy land harder than a loud interface would.
2. **Fire is a scalpel, not a bucket.** The orange→red gradient marks only what earns it: the streak flame, the primary CTA, live progress. Everywhere else is dark and quiet so the accent actually means something.
3. **Screenshottable by default.** Every screen should look good dropped into a group chat. That is the acquisition engine, so it is a design constraint, not a nice-to-have.
4. **Behavior is the hero, chrome recedes.** Status, streaks, and progress are the loudest elements; navigation and containers step back.
5. **Roast the behavior, never the person.** Tone in both copy and visual framing targets the excuse and the flake — never body, identity, or worth.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Dark theme with genuinely high contrast — body text ≥4.5:1 against its surface (no light-gray-for-elegance on near-black). Status is never conveyed by color alone: every status badge carries a text label (Done / Due / Skipped / Off). Reduced-motion alternatives for every animation (crossfade or instant). Thumb-friendly targets (≥40px) for a one-handed mobile app.
